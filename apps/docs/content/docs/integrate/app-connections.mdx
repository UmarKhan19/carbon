Carbon ships a set of first-party **integrations** to third-party apps. Each one is enabled per company under **Settings ﻗ Integrations**, where you flip it on and supply its credentials or settings.

## Available integrations

| Integration | Category | What it does |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Email | Email | Sends outbound email ﻗ Carbon-managed via Resend, or your own SMTP server. |
| Exchange Rates | Accounting | Keeps currency exchange rates up to date automatically. |
| Xero | Accounting | Posts sales and purchase invoices to Xero, with account mapping and an initial backfill. |
| Slack | Assistant | Brings the Carbon Assistant into Slack and sends nonconformance notifications. |
| Onshape | CAD | Syncs CAD data from Onshape. |
| Paperless Parts | Estimating & Quoting | Syncs quotes and BOMs with Paperless Parts. |
| Jira | Project Management | Links quality issues to Jira. |
| Linear | Project Management | Syncs issues with Linear. |

<Callout type="note">
Xero, Slack, Onshape, and Jira authenticate over OAuth; Exchange Rates, Linear, and Paperless Parts use an API key; Email is configured with provider settings. A few connectors need server-side credentials to appear at all ﻗ **Jira** and **Onshape** require their OAuth client to be configured, and **Slack** is unavailable in controlled (ITAR) environments.
</Callout>

## How configuration works

An integration has two parts: a system-wide definition and a per-company activation.

- The **definition** carries a JSON schema describing the fields that integration needs (API keys, OAuth tokens, mapping options).
- The **activation** is a `companyIntegration` row ﻗ an `active` flag plus a `metadata` object holding your credentials and settings. Carbon validates that metadata against the integration's schema, so an integration can't be switched on with incomplete configuration.

That's the whole model: turning an integration on is writing a validated `metadata` blob and setting `active = true` for your company.

## How syncing works

Integrations that mirror data ﻗ Xero, Linear, Jira, Paperless Parts ﻗ keep a mapping between each Carbon record and its counterpart in the external system, so a Carbon customer, item, or invoice always resolves to the same remote id on the next sync. The work itself runs in the background: an initial backfill when you connect, then continuous syncing as records change.
