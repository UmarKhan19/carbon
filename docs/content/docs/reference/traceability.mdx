title: Traceability
description: Serial and batch tracking, and the genealogy that links what went into what.

For items that need it, Carbon tracks individual units and lots as **<Term id="tracked-entity">tracked entities</Term>**, and records every consume-and-produce as it happens, so you can prove what went into a finished unit, and trace a suspect lot forward to everything it touched.

## Tracked entities

A **tracked entity** is a tracked quantity of an item. A **<Term id="serial">Serial</Term>** item gets one entity per unit (quantity 1); a **<Term id="batch">Batch</Term>** item gets one entity per lot. The serial or batch number, and where it came from, live on the entity's attributes. An entity carries a status as it moves:

<StatusFlow entity="trackedEntity">
  <Status name="Available">In stock, free to pick or consume.</Status>
  <Status name="Reserved" branch>Allocated: e.g. a job's output before it's made.</Status>
  <Status name="On Hold" branch>Received but not yet released (awaiting inspection).</Status>
  <Status name="Consumed" accent>Issued to a job or shipped out.</Status>
  <Status name="Rejected" terminal>Failed inspection.</Status>
</StatusFlow>

## Where entities come from

Receiving a tracked item creates entities **On Hold**; posting the receipt releases them to **Available**, unless the item needs inspection. Then they stay On Hold until it clears, or become **Rejected** if it fails. On the make side, a job reserves its output entity up front and flips it to **Available** when the unit is produced.

## Genealogy

Every material event (consume, produce, split, ship) is recorded as an **activity** with **inputs** (what was consumed) and **outputs** (what was produced). Parent and child link through the activity, so the <Term>genealogy</Term> graph is built as work happens and walked later to trace a unit's ancestry or descendants. Consuming part of a batch records a **split**, linking the original lot to the consumed portion and the remainder.

<Callout type="note">
There are no separate serial-number or batch-number tables — one tracked-entity model represents both, and the number itself is an attribute. Genealogy isn't a stored parent pointer; it's reconstructed by walking the activity graph from a unit outward.
</Callout>

## Shelf life

Tracked items can carry a **shelf life**: a fixed duration, a date set at receipt, or one calculated from the earliest-expiring component consumed. Carbon stamps the resulting expiration date on the entity, and picking favors the earliest expiry first (FEFO). Shelf life applies only to Serial and Batch items. There has to be a per-unit record to stamp.
