---
title: Jobs
description: One run of production — a single item, a quantity to make, and its own copy of the method.
---

A **job** is the unit of production work in Carbon. It pins down one item, how many to make, where, and by when — and carries its own copy of the <Term id="method">manufacturing method</Term> (the materials and operations) so the floor always builds from a fixed recipe, even as the part master changes underneath it.

Everything on the shop floor hangs off a job: the schedule board places its operations, material is <Term id="material-issue">issued</Term> against it, time is logged to it, and cost accumulates into its <Term id="wip">work-in-process</Term>. A <Term id="sales-order">sales order</Term> line that's made to order becomes a job; planning turns a stock shortfall into one. It's the bridge between demand and the floor.

## Fields

<FieldTable>
  <Field name="Item" type="reference">What's being made. The job copies this item's method on release.</Field>
  <Field name="Quantity" type="number">Good units to produce.</Field>
  <Field name="Scrap quantity" type="number">Units expected lost; `production quantity` = quantity + scrap.</Field>
  <Field name="Quantity complete" type="number">Good units finished so far.</Field>
  <Field name="Location" type="reference">The site the job runs at and receives into.</Field>
  <Field name="Due date" type="date">When it's needed; paired with a deadline type that tells the scheduler how hard the date is.</Field>
  <Field name="Source" type="reference">The sales order line or quote line it was raised for, if any.</Field>
  <Field name="Assignee" type="reference">The person responsible for the job.</Field>
</FieldTable>

## Status lifecycle

A job's status drives what you can do with it and what the floor sees.

<StatusFlow entity="job">
  <Status name="Draft">Created and editable; not yet planned or scheduled.</Status>
  <Status name="Planned">Demand is visible to planning, but no work is on the board yet.</Status>
  <Status name="Ready" accent>Released: operations are scheduled and material requirements raised.</Status>
  <Status name="In Progress">The first production event has been logged against it.</Status>
  <Status name="Paused" branch>Work is temporarily halted.</Status>
  <Status name="Completed">Every operation is done; finished goods have been received into inventory.</Status>
  <Status name="Closed">The books are settled. Any residual work-in-process is swept to variance.</Status>
  <Status name="Cancelled" terminal>Abandoned before completion.</Status>
</StatusFlow>

<Callout type="info">
A **Completed**, **Closed**, or **Cancelled** job is locked. Its method, quantity, and dates can no longer be edited. Closing is what makes a job's work-in-process provably zero.
</Callout>

## Make to order vs. make to stock

A job is identical whichever way it's raised. Only its source differs. A **<Term id="make-to-order">make-to-order</Term>** job links back to a sales order line; a **make-to-stock** job comes from <Term id="mrp">planning</Term> with no order behind it. Either way the job completes into inventory first, and the sale (if any) ships from stock — the job never ships directly to a customer.

<Callout type="note">
Releasing a job copies the part's method into a job-specific copy. Editing that copy never touches the part master, and updating the part never disturbs a job already in flight.
</Callout>
