title: Purchase orders

A **purchase order** is a commitment to buy from a supplier. It may come from comparing <Term id="supplier-quote">supplier quotes</Term> or be raised directly, and it's the document <Term id="receipt">receipts</Term> and <Term id="invoice">supplier invoices</Term> reconcile against.

Like a <Term id="sales-order">sales order</Term> in reverse, a purchase order line keeps two independent counters: how much has been received, and how much has been invoiced. It only closes when both are satisfied. Receiving and billing advance the same order along separate axes.

## Line fields

<FieldTable>
  <Field name="Item" type="reference">What's being bought.</Field>
  <Field name="Purchase quantity" type="number">Ordered quantity.</Field>
  <Field name="Quantity received" type="number">Received so far; `quantity to receive` is the remainder.</Field>
  <Field name="Quantity invoiced" type="number">Billed so far; `quantity to invoice` is the remainder.</Field>
  <Field name="Conversion factor" type="number">Converts the supplier's purchase unit to the stock unit.</Field>
</FieldTable>

## Types

A purchase order is one of three kinds: a plain **Purchase**, a **Return** to the supplier, or **<Term id="outside-operation">Outside Processing</Term>** — sending parts out to a vendor for a production step, where the line links to a job operation.

## Status

The status is computed from the state of its lines, never set by hand.
