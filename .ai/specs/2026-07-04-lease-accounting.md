## TLDR

Carbon has **zero lease accounting code** (verified 2026-07-04: `grep -ri lease` across migrations and `modules/accounting` yields only false positives — "release", the Romania ISO code `ROU`). This spec builds a lease subledger beside fixed assets in the accounting module: a lease master with payment structures, IBR, and term options; PV computation producing an ROU asset + lease liability with **effective-interest schedules**; ASC 842 operating/finance classification with the correct expense pattern per class; short-term and low-value practical expedients; **modifications and remeasurements** that re-spread schedules from the effective date; **lessor accounting** (operating / sales-type / direct financing) with full schema, operating-lessor income posting in v1, and net-investment interest posting in v1 (commencement derecognition JE is system-drafted, user-posted — see Lessor section); monthly lease journals generated by a scheduled Inngest run and posted as `accounting`-source entries under the period matrix; a **"Post lease journals"** close-checklist task with an auto-check on due unposted schedule lines; disclosure data (maturity analysis, weighted-average term and discount rate, cash paid); and **IFRS 16 single-model treatment as an adjustment-book delta** consuming the multi-book generator framework (`.ai/specs/2026-07-04-multi-book.md`, parallel spec on this branch).

## Problem Statement

ASC 842 / IFRS 16 put essentially all leases on the balance sheet. A public-company (or audit-ready) manufacturer with facility, equipment, and vehicle leases must produce, per period and per book: ROU asset and lease liability balances tied to effective-interest schedules, the correct expense pattern (single straight-line lease cost for ASC 842 operating leases; interest + amortization for finance leases and *all* IFRS 16 leases), remeasured schedules after modifications, and the ASC 842-20-50 / IFRS 16 ¶51+ disclosure package (maturity analysis by year, weighted-average remaining term and discount rate, cash paid for leases). Carbon has none of this; the interim path (FinQuery-style JE upload via GAP-E1) leaves no subledger tying to the GL. Manufacturers also **lease out** equipment (lessor side) — the ambitious scope Brad approved 2026-07-04 includes lessor classification and posting, not just lessee.

## Proposed Solution

### 1. Lease master

One `lease` table covers both roles (`role`: `Lessee` | `Lessor`) — counterparty is `supplierId` (lessee) or `customerId` (lessor). Core fields: readable `leaseId` (sequence `lease`), `leaseClassId` (GL mapping + defaults), commencement date, non-cancellable end date, renewal/termination/purchase options (own table, each with a "reasonably certain" flag that extends the accounting term), IBR (`discountRate`, annual %, converted to periodic at computation), payment frequency (`Monthly`/`Quarterly`/`Annual`), timing (`Advance`/`Arrears`), currency, initial direct costs, incentives received, prepaid rent — all feeding ROU = liability + IDC + prepaid − incentives. Classification inputs live on the lease: underlying-asset fair value, remaining economic life (months), ownership-transfer and specialized-asset flags, residual value guaranteed. Status: `Draft` → `Active` (activation computes schedule v1 and posts/drafts the commencement JE) → `Terminated`/`Expired`.

**Payment structure**, not a flat amount: `leasePaymentTerm` rows (date range, amount per period, optional escalation % per annum) generate the dated fixed cashflows; in-substance-fixed payments are entered as fixed. **Variable payments** (usage/sales-based, and index-linked *changes*) are never in the liability — `leaseVariablePayment` rows are recorded as incurred and expensed by the monthly run (ASC 842: index-linked payments stay measured at the commencement index; rate changes hit variable expense. IFRS 16 remeasures on index change — handled as a remeasurement event, see §4, and flagged as IFRS-book-relevant).

### 2. PV computation, schedules, classification

`buildLeaseSchedule()` (in `accounting.utils.ts`, beside `buildDepreciationLines()`) discounts the fixed cashflows at the periodic IBR and emits one `leaseScheduleLine` per period carrying **both expense patterns** so later books/reclassifications never recompute: `openingLiability`, `paymentAmount`, `interestAmount`, `principalAmount`, `closingLiability`, `rouAmortizationFinance` (straight-line ROU/term), `leaseCostOperating` (total cost/term), `rouAmortizationOperating` (plug = operating cost − interest). The last line absorbs rounding so the liability closes to exactly 0.

**Classification (ASC 842-10-25-2)** is computed at activation and stored with its inputs: (a) ownership transfers; (b) purchase option reasonably certain; (c) term ≥ major part of remaining economic life (policy threshold, default **75%**); (d) PV of payments + guaranteed residual ≥ substantially all of fair value (default **90%**); (e) specialized asset. Any test true ⇒ `Finance`, else `Operating`. Thresholds live on `companySettings`; a manual override with required reason is allowed and audit-logged.

**Practical expedients**: `isShortTerm` (term ≤ 12 months, no reasonably-certain purchase option — policy election on `companySettings`, default on) and `isLowValue` (IFRS-only election, threshold default $5,000) bypass capitalization entirely: no liability/ROU, straight-line expense line in the monthly run, and separate disclosure buckets for cash paid.

**Worked example (acceptance baseline).** 36-month lease, $10,000/month in arrears, 6% annual IBR (0.5%/month): initial liability = 10,000 × (1 − 1.005⁻³⁶)/0.005 = **$328,710.16** = initial ROU (no IDC/incentives). Month 1: interest **$1,643.55**, principal **$8,356.45**, closing liability **$320,353.71**. Finance pattern month 1: interest $1,643.55 + ROU amortization $9,130.84 (= 328,710.16/36) = $10,774.39 expense. Operating pattern month 1: single lease cost $10,000.00, ROU amortization plug $8,356.45.

### 3. Journal patterns (lessee)

| Event | Finance | Operating |
|---|---|---|
| Commencement | Dr ROU asset / Cr Lease liability (+ Cr Cash for IDC, Dr for incentives) | same |
| Monthly — payment | Dr Lease liability (principal) + Dr Interest expense / Cr AP‑lease clearing | Dr Operating lease expense (leaseCostOperating) / Cr AP‑lease clearing (payment); Dr Lease liability (principal) / Cr Accumulated ROU amortization (plug) |
| Monthly — amortization | Dr ROU amortization expense / Cr Accumulated ROU amortization (SL) | (in the plug above — single expense line preserved) |
| Variable / short-term / low-value | Dr Variable (or Short-term) lease expense / Cr AP‑lease clearing | same |

The credit leg posts to a **lease clearing account** relieved by the existing AP invoice/payment flow (leases are invoiced by real suppliers); companies not entering supplier invoices for rent can map clearing = cash. `journalEntrySourceType` gains `'Lease'` (precedent: `'Asset Depreciation'`).

### 4. Modifications and remeasurements

`leaseEvent` records every reassessment with `reason` (`Term Change`, `Payment Change`, `Index Change (IFRS)`, `Option Reassessment`, `Partial Termination`, `Full Termination`), effective date, revised inputs, and the liability before/after. Processing: schedule lines from the effective period forward are superseded (`scheduleVersion` bump; old lines kept, flagged), the liability is re-PV'd at the **revised** IBR when required (term/option changes) or the original rate (index changes under IFRS), and ROU is adjusted by the liability delta. Partial termination reduces ROU proportionately and posts a gain/loss; full termination derecognizes both and posts gain/loss. Modifications that add an ROU at market rate for an additional asset are a **new lease** (per both standards) — the UI offers "create linked lease". Posted history is never mutated — remeasurement affects only unposted future lines plus one adjustment JE at the effective date (period-close-safe by construction).

### 5. Lessor accounting (the ambitious part)

Same `lease` table, `role = 'Lessor'`, own classification enum (`Operating` / `Sales-Type` / `Direct Financing`) using the mirrored ASC 842-10-25-2/-3 tests (sales-type: any lessee-finance criterion; direct financing: PV + third-party residual guarantee ≥ substantially all + collection probable).

- **Operating lessor — full posting in v1.** Underlying asset stays on `fixedAsset` and depreciates normally (existing runs; the lease links `fixedAssetId`). Monthly run posts straight-line lease income: Dr Lease receivable (clearing → AR invoice) / Cr Lease income; uneven cash vs straight-line income accrues to a straight-line rent receivable/liability account on the class.
- **Sales-type / direct financing — schema + schedules + interest posting in v1; commencement derecognition system-drafted, user-posted.** The schedule engine reuses `leaseScheduleLine` with lessor semantics (`openingLiability` ≡ opening net investment; `interestAmount` ≡ interest income; residual accretes via a final unguaranteed-residual row). The **monthly interest-income accrual posts automatically in v1** (same poster, Dr Net investment / Cr Interest income; Dr Lease clearing / Cr Net investment for cash). The **commencement JE** (derecognize inventory/asset at carrying amount, recognize net investment, selling profit/COGS for sales-type) is generated as a **Draft journal proposal** the user reviews and posts, because automated derecognition must integrate with item costing/inventory relief that GAP-3 (standard cost, revaluation) has not landed yet — auto-posting a COGS relief against an unfinished costing layer would corrupt inventory valuation. This is a schema-now/auto-post-later cut on **one JE only**; everything downstream (net-investment schedule, interest income, receipts) posts automatically in v1.

### 6. Monthly run + close checklist

An Inngest cron (`packages/jobs`, monthly, per company — pattern GAP-4.1 will reuse for depreciation) creates a Draft **`leaseRun`** (`leaseRunId` readable, `periodEnd`, `status` `Draft|Posted` — mirrors `depreciationRun`) with one `leaseRunLine` per due item: unposted `leaseScheduleLine`s with period ≤ periodEnd, variable payments, short-term/low-value expense lines, operating-lessor income. `postLeaseRun()` (in `accounting.server.ts`, beside `postDepreciationRun()`) posts per lease with `getOrCreateAccountingPeriod(..., source: "accounting")`, stamps `journalId` on lines, and marks schedule lines Posted. Manual "create run now" button exists (depreciation-run parity).

**Close checklist registration**: seed a `periodCloseTaskDefinition` — name **"Post lease journals"**, type Auto, `autoCheckKey: 'unposted-lease-schedules'`, severity **Warning** (parity with "Post depreciation runs"), ordered directly after it. The evaluator flags any Active lease with unposted schedule lines (or an unposted Draft `leaseRun`) dated in the period.

### 7. IFRS 16 delta via adjustment book

Key insight: ASC 842 operating leases are **already capitalized** — the IFRS 16 difference is expense pattern only, no balance-sheet reclass. The multi-book spec's generator framework gets a registered `ifrs16-lease` generator that, for each posted operating-lease period, emits into the IFRS adjustment book: Dr Interest expense (`interestAmount`) + Dr ROU amortization expense (`rouAmortizationFinance`) / Cr Operating lease expense (`leaseCostOperating`), net P&L delta = the front-loading difference. Zero recomputation — both patterns are persisted on every schedule line (§2). Finance leases and lessor leases generate no delta. IFRS-only remeasurements (index changes, low-value threshold differences) post book-specific `leaseEvent` adjustments through the same generator. If multi-book ships later than leases, the generator registers then; nothing here blocks on it.

### 8. Disclosures

Service-computed, no new posting: **maturity analysis** (undiscounted remaining fixed payments by each of the next 5 fiscal years + thereafter, less imputed interest, reconciling to the liability — lessor mirror for net investment), **weighted-average remaining lease term** and **weighted-average discount rate** (liability-weighted, split operating/finance), **cash paid** (operating-lease operating cash, finance principal = financing, finance interest = operating, plus short-term/variable buckets), ROU by class. Rendered on a Leases report route with CSV export (table-csv-export pattern).

### Design Decisions

| Decision | Choice | Rationale |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| GL account mapping | **`leaseClass` table** (Real Estate / Equipment / Vehicles seeded), NOT `accountDefault` columns | `fixedAssetClass` precedent is exact: per-class NOT NULL account FKs, seeded classes, class-level defaults. Leases need ~11 accounts × per-class variation (real-estate ROU vs equipment ROU); `accountDefault` is one row per company and already wide — 11 more columns there loses per-class mapping and violates the sibling subledger's pattern. |
| One `lease` table for both roles | `role` column + role-specific nullable columns + CHECK constraints, two classification enums | Shared 80% (payments, term, IBR, schedules, events, runs); separate tables would duplicate the schedule/event/run machinery. CHECKs keep role integrity (lessee ⇒ `supplierId`, lessor ⇒ `customerId`). |
| Schedule stores both expense patterns | `rouAmortizationFinance`, `leaseCostOperating`, `rouAmortizationOperating` on every line | Makes the IFRS 16 generator a pure reader; reclassification-on-modification re-spreads without recomputing history; costs 3 numeric columns. |
| Remeasurement model | Versioned schedule (supersede future lines), never mutate posted lines | Posted-journal immutability (period-closing MW-1 fold-in) makes in-place edits impossible anyway; versions give the auditor the before/after. |
| Lessor sales-type commencement JE | System-drafted, user-posted in v1; auto-post after GAP-3 costing lands | Auto derecognition needs inventory relief at correct carrying cost; standard costing is dead schema today (GAP-3). One manual-review JE vs corrupting inventory valuation. Interest schedules + posting are fully automatic in v1. |
| Monthly posting vehicle | `leaseRun`/`leaseRunLine` Draft→Posted batch, Inngest-proposed | `depreciationRun` precedent; approvable batch matches MW-2 approval direction; scheduled proposal fixes the "entirely manual" gotcha fixed assets still have. |
| Source type | Single `'Lease'` `journalEntrySourceType` value | Terminations/modifications are distinguishable via the lease/event FK on the run line; enum values are irreversible, add sparingly. |
| Multi-tenancy (heuristic 1) | Every new table: `companyId`, composite PK `("id","companyId")`, `id('prefix')` defaults, audit columns, `customFields` on master tables | House convention; child FKs reference composites (tax-spec precedent). |
| Service shape (heuristic 2) | All functions in `accounting.service.ts` / models in `accounting.models.ts`; Kysely transactions in `accounting.server.ts` (`postLeaseRun`, `activateLease`, `processLeaseEvent`); calc in `accounting.utils.ts`; `(client, companyId, ...)` → `{data, error}` | Leases live beside fixed assets in the accounting module — one service file per module, no new module. |
| RLS (heuristic 3) | Four standard policies per table: SELECT `get_companies_with_employee_role()` (or `_permission('accounting_view')` matching fixed-asset tables), writes `get_companies_with_employee_permission('accounting_*')` | AR/AP-payments migration pattern (`20260630093809`). |
| Permissions (heuristic 4) | Routes `view/create/update/delete: "accounting"`; posting `update: "accounting"` | Fixed-asset and depreciation-run route precedent exactly. |
| Forms (heuristic 5) | `ValidatedForm` + zod (`leaseValidator`, `leaseClassValidator`, `leasePaymentTermValidator`, `leaseEventValidator`, `leaseRunValidator`); Drawer overlays for detail | House convention. |
| Module layout (heuristic 6) | `modules/accounting/ui/Leases/`; routes `x+/lease+/` + `x+/lease-run+/` + list pages under `x+/accounting+/` | Mirrors `FixedAssets/` + `fixed-asset+/` + `depreciation-run+/`. |
| Backward compatibility (heuristic 7) | Purely additive: new tables, one enum value, `companySettings`/checklist-seed additions; no existing surface changes | No frozen surface touched; companies without leases see one new nav item and one auto-passing close task. |

- [ ] Same lease classified **Operating** (tests all false) posts a single $10,000.00 lease cost and reduces liability 8,356.45 / accumulated ROU amortization 8,356.45; total expense over 36 months equals $360,000.00 under both classifications.
- [ ] Payment-timing Advance and quarterly frequency produce correct PV (annuity-due / quarterly rate) — unit tests on `buildLeaseSchedule()`.
- [ ] A 12-month lease with the short-term expedient creates no liability/ROU and posts straight-line expense via run lines of type `Short-Term`.
- [ ] **Modification**: extending the example lease by 12 months at month 12 with a revised rate creates schedule v2 from month 13, marks unposted v1 lines superseded (posted lines untouched), posts one ROU/liability adjustment JE equal to the liability delta, and the v2 schedule closes to $0.00.
- [ ] **Partial termination** reduces ROU proportionately and posts the gain/loss; **full termination** derecognizes both balances.
- [ ] **Lessor operating**: monthly run posts Dr Lease clearing / Cr Lease income straight-line; uneven payment terms accrue to the straight-line rent account. **Lessor sales-type**: activation generates a Draft commencement JE (not auto-posted) and a net-investment schedule; monthly interest income posts automatically; net investment closes to unguaranteed residual at term end.

- [ ] Close checklist for a period with due unposted schedule lines shows "Post lease journals" failing (Warning, skippable with reason); it passes after posting.
- [ ] Disclosure service: maturity analysis buckets undiscounted payments by the next 5 fiscal years + thereafter and reconciles (total − imputed interest = liability balance); weighted-average term and rate computed liability-weighted split by classification; cash-paid summary splits operating/financing/short-term/variable.
- [ ] IFRS 16 generator (`ifrs16-lease`) registered per the multi-book spec emits, for a posted operating-lease month, an adjustment-book JE reclassifying 10,000.00 operating cost into 1,643.55 interest + 9,130.84 amortization (net delta 774.39 in month 1); no delta for finance leases.
